London overview

Build the plan around location and journey time

London is a large city with major visitor areas spread across several transport zones. A practical itinerary usually works better when activities are grouped by area rather than selected in isolation.

  • List the places that matter most to your group.
  • Check opening arrangements with each attraction.
  • Group nearby activities into the same day.
  • Leave additional time for interchanges and queues.

JA planning approach

Start with your priorities, mobility requirements and available time. Then compare areas, transport routes and provider terms before committing to paid activities.

Getting around

Use official journey planning for live routes

London’s transport network includes Underground, Elizabeth line, rail, bus, DLR, tram, river and walking connections. Live disruption and accessibility information should be checked directly with Transport for London.

Plan journeys before each day

Compare routes using the official TfL journey planner and recheck them shortly before travel. Engineering work, closures and events can affect the most obvious route.

Open TfL Journey Planner

Check accessibility carefully

Step-free access varies by station and route. Review the official accessibility information for every important journey rather than assuming an entire line is step-free.

TfL accessibility guidance

Airport connections

London is served by several airports, each with different rail, coach, Underground and road connections. Choose accommodation only after checking the relevant airport journey.

Fares and payment

Review current TfL information about contactless payment, Oyster and travelcards. Fares and eligibility can change, so avoid relying on an old price quoted by a third party.

Accommodation-area research

Consider the area before the property

JA Group Services Ltd does not sell accommodation. These examples are planning prompts to help customers research locations with external providers.

Westminster and Victoria

Worth researching for access to many central landmarks and Victoria transport connections. Compare street noise, walking distance and the exact station entrance.

South Bank and Waterloo

Useful to research for riverside attractions and Waterloo connections. Check which side of the river best fits the rest of your itinerary.

King’s Cross and Bloomsbury

Consider for major rail links and access towards central London. Check the immediate neighbourhood and onward route to evening plans.

City of London

May suit plans focused on the Tower, St Paul’s and eastern central areas. Weekend and evening atmosphere can differ from working weekdays.

Kensington and Paddington

Research for museums, western central London and selected airport connections. Compare exact travel time rather than relying on an area name.

Outside central London

Can offer different space or pricing, but calculate daily travel time and fare implications before deciding that it represents better value.
